Spring

Spring is a season of new life. Plants sprout from the earth, flowers bloom, and animals are born. But even more fully, new life is found in the resurrected Christ. It is a time to realize the saving power of God in our own lives, and to receive His redemptive gift ever more keenly.

Summer

Summer is a time for the continued flourishing of life. It is also the hottest season, which allows people to go outdoors and travel more easily. On a spiritual level, after we have received the joyful redemption of Easter, the Holy Spirit descends on us, as in the summer holiday of Pentacost, allowing us to carry the Gospel outward.

Autumn

Autumn is a time of preparation. The squirrels store up there nuts for the winter, and the bears take to their dens. They anticipate not only the trial of the winter, but also the renewal of the spring. So should we prepare for a spiritual winter, storing up treasure in heaven, and gathering up God's grace so as to prepare for the times when we have only faith to cling to. This preparation is especially reflected in the liturgical season of Advent, which occures at the end of Autumn and points toward Christ's coming.

Winter

Winter is marked by desolation and perseverence. Many plants recede into the ground, along with some animals, and migratory animals have to leave their summer home. They do these things so that they can partake more fully in the fruit of Spring when it comes. All of us will enter into spiritual winters whether prepared or not. Lent, which begins during the winter, upholds the spiritual disciplines proper to winter time so that we can more readily absorb the grace of Easter.
